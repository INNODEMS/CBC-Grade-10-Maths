2. **Do (Procedural Skill):** Form quadratic expressions by multiplying monomials and binomials using the distributive property and collecting like terms.  
3. **Apply (Application/Problem-Solving):** Apply algebraic manipulation to simplify expressions and form quadratic expressions from different situations.

# **IV. Lesson Procedure**

## **Phase 1: Problem-Solving and Discovery / Engage & Explore (15 minutes)**

**Learning Outcome:** To activate prior knowledge about algebraic expressions and explore the structure of quadratic expressions.

**Anchor Learning Experience: Exploring Quadratic Expressions**

Work in Groups:

- Identify and label the terms of the quadratic expression: quadratic term, linear term, and constant term.  
- Discuss whether the expression is in standard form and, if not, try to convert it.  
- Identify the coefficients (a, b) and constant (c).  
- Discuss the properties of the expression: whether it can be factored, whether it has real solutions, etc.  
- Present your findings to the class.

**Examples of Quadratic Expressions for Groups:**

* Group 1: 2x² \+ 5x − 3  
* Group 2: 3x² − 4x \+ 1  
* Group 3: x² \+ 6x \+ 9  
* Group 4: 4x² − 9

**Teacher's Role:** The teacher circulates among the groups, asking probing questions (e.g., "Which term has x²?", "What is the coefficient of x?", "What happens if a \= 0?"). The teacher uses student discoveries to bridge to formal instruction on forming quadratic expressions.

## **Phase 2: Structured Instruction / Explain (10 minutes)**

**Learning Outcome:** To formalize the definition and structure of quadratic expressions and methods to form them.

**Key Takeaways:**

**Definition of a Quadratic Expression:**

A quadratic expression is an expression of the form:

**ax² \+ bx \+ c**

Where:

• a, b, and c are constants (real numbers)

• x is a variable

• a ≠ 0 (if a \= 0, it would not be a quadratic expression)

**Parts of a Quadratic Expression:**

| Term | Example (2x² \+ 5x − 3\) | Description |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Quadratic Term | 2x² | The term with x² (coefficient a \= 2\) |
| Linear Term | 5x | The term with x (coefficient b \= 5\) |
| Constant Term | −3 | The term without x (c \= −3) |

**Types of Algebraic Expressions:**

• Monomial: An expression with one term (e.g., 3x, y², 5\)

• Binomial: An expression with two terms (e.g., (a \+ b), (x − 3))

**Forming Quadratic Expressions:**

Quadratic expressions can be formed by:

1\. Multiplying a monomial by a binomial (using distributive property)

2\. Multiplying two binomials

**The Distributive Property:**

When multiplying a monomial by a binomial, multiply the monomial by each term in the binomial, then combine like terms.

Example: a(b \+ c) \= ab \+ ac

**Addressing Misconceptions:** "Remember: When distributing a negative sign, it changes the sign of each term inside the bracket. Also, always collect like terms at the end to simplify your expression."

## **Phase 3: Practice and Application / Elaborate (15 minutes)**

**Learning Outcome:** To apply the distributive property and form quadratic expressions by simplifying algebraic expressions.

**Worked Example:**

Simplify: 2a(a − 1\) − 3(a² − 1\)

Solution:

Step 1: Open the brackets using distributive property

2a(a − 1\) − 3(a² − 1\)

\= 2a(a) \+ 2a(−1) \+ (−3)(a²) \+ (−3)(−1)

\= 2a² − 2a − 3a² \+ 3

Step 2: Collect like terms

\= 2a² − 3a² − 2a \+ 3

\= −a² − 2a \+ 3

**Note:** In this example, (2a) and (−3) are monomial expressions, while (a − 1\) and (a² − 1\) are binomial expressions.

**Teacher's Role:** The teacher monitors students, emphasizing careful sign handling when distributing negative terms.

## **Phase 4: Assessment / Evaluate (Exit Ticket)**

**Learning Outcome:** To formatively assess individual student understanding.

**Exit Ticket Questions:**

Form a quadratic expression by simplifying:

1\. 3x(x \+ 2\) − 4(x² − 1\)

2\. 8n(n \+ 5\) − 3(n² − 6\)

3\. 4p(p − 1\) − 5(p² \+ 2\)

4\. 7a(a \+ 3\) − 2(a² − 2\)

**Answer Key:**

1\. 3x(x \+ 2\) − 4(x² − 1\)

   \= 3x² \+ 6x − 4x² \+ 4

   \= −x² \+ 6x \+ 4

2\. 8n(n \+ 5\) − 3(n² − 6\)

   \= 8n² \+ 40n − 3n² \+ 18

   \= 5n² \+ 40n \+ 18

3\. 4p(p − 1\) − 5(p² \+ 2\)

   \= 4p² − 4p − 5p² − 10

   \= −p² − 4p − 10

4\. 7a(a \+ 3\) − 2(a² − 2\)

   \= 7a² \+ 21a − 2a² \+ 4

   \= 5a² \+ 21a \+ 4

# **V. Differentiation**

| Student Group | Strategy & Learning Experience |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Struggling Learners (Support)** | Scaffolding: Provide step-by-step templates showing the distribution process. Start with simpler expressions (one monomial × one binomial). Use color coding to track terms. Allow peer support during practice. |
| **On-Level Learners (Core)** | The core lesson activities as described above. |
| **Advanced Learners (Challenge)** | Extended Learning Experience: Simplify more complex expressions such as: a) 2m − 2(m − 1)² b) (x \+ 2)(x − 3\) \+ x(x \+ 1\) c) Create a real-world problem that results in a quadratic expression. |

## **Extended Learning Experience Solutions:**

a) 2m − 2(m − 1)²

   \= 2m − 2(m² − 2m \+ 1\)

   \= 2m − 2m² \+ 4m − 2

   \= −2m² \+ 6m − 2

b) (x \+ 2)(x − 3\) \+ x(x \+ 1\)

   \= x² − 3x \+ 2x − 6 \+ x² \+ x

   \= 2x² \+ 0x − 6

   \= 2x² − 6
