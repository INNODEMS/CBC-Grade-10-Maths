## **Learning Outcomes**

Upon completion of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. **Know (Conceptual Understanding):** Understand that logarithms convert multiplication to addition, division to subtraction, and powers/roots to multiplication/division of logarithms.  
2. **Do (Procedural Skill):** Use logarithm tables to perform multiplication, division, and find powers and roots of numbers.  
3. **Apply (Application/Problem-Solving):** Apply logarithmic calculations to solve real-world problems involving areas, volumes, and compound calculations.

# **IV. Lesson Procedure**

## **Phase 1: Problem-Solving and Discovery / Engage & Explore (15 minutes)**

**Learning Outcome:** To explore how logarithms simplify complex calculations.

**Anchor Learning Experience: The Calculator Challenge**

Work in groups to explore the following challenge:

**Part A: The Long Way**

Without using a calculator, try to compute:

1\. 236.5 × 42.8 \= ?

2\. 528.6 ÷ 24.7 \= ?

3\. ∛524.8 \= ?

**Part B: Discovering the Pattern**

Use your logarithm tables to find:

• log(236.5) \= ?

• log(42.8) \= ?

• What do you notice when you ADD these two logarithms?

• What happens when you find the antilog of the sum?

**Discussion Questions:**

* How does adding logarithms relate to multiplication?  
* How does subtracting logarithms relate to division?  
* What operation on logarithms gives you a power?  
* What operation on logarithms gives you a root?

**Teacher's Role:** Circulate among groups, asking probing questions. Guide students to discover that log(a × b) \= log(a) \+ log(b) and log(a ÷ b) \= log(a) \- log(b). Use student discoveries to bridge to formal instruction.

## **Phase 2: Structured Instruction / Explain (10 minutes)**

**Learning Outcome:** To formalize the laws of logarithms for multiplication, division, powers, and roots.

**Key Takeaways:**

**The Four Laws of Logarithms for Calculations:**

1\. Product Law (Multiplication):

   log(a × b) \= log(a) \+ log(b)

   To multiply: ADD the logarithms, then find antilog

2\. Quotient Law (Division):

   log(a ÷ b) \= log(a) \- log(b)

   To divide: SUBTRACT the logarithms, then find antilog

3\. Power Law:

   log(aᵇ) \= b × log(a)

   To find a power: MULTIPLY the logarithm by the exponent, then find antilog

4\. Root Law:

   log(ⁿ√a) \= (1/n) × log(a)

   To find a root: DIVIDE the logarithm by the root index, then find antilog

**The General Process:**

Step 1: Find log of each number from tables

Step 2: Apply the appropriate operation (+, \-, ×, ÷)

Step 3: Find antilog of the result

**Addressing Misconceptions:** "Remember: log(a \+ b) ≠ log(a) \+ log(b). The laws only work for multiplication, division, powers, and roots \- NOT for addition or subtraction\!"

## **Phase 3: Practice and Application / Elaborate (15 minutes)**

**Learning Outcome:** To apply logarithm laws to perform calculations.

**Worked Example 1: Multiplication**

Find 236.5 × 42.8 using logarithm tables.

Solution:

Step 1: Find logarithms from tables

   log(236.5) \= 2.3741

   log(42.8) \= 1.6318

Step 2: Add the logarithms

   2.3741 \+ 1.6318 \= 4.0059

Step 3: Find antilogarithm

   antilog(4.0059) ≈ 10,160

**Therefore: 236.5 × 42.8 ≈ 10,160**

**Worked Example 2: Division**

Find 528.6 ÷ 24.7 using logarithm tables.

Solution:

Step 1: Find logarithms from tables

   log(528.6) \= 2.7233

   log(24.7) \= 1.3927

Step 2: Subtract the logarithms

   2.7233 \- 1.3927 \= 1.3306

Step 3: Find antilogarithm

   antilog(1.3306) ≈ 21.4

**Therefore: 528.6 ÷ 24.7 ≈ 21.4**

**Worked Example 3: Cube Root**

Evaluate ∛524.8 using logarithm tables.

Solution:

Step 1: Find logarithm

   log(524.8) \= 2.7200

Step 2: Divide by 3 (cube root)

   2.7200 ÷ 3 \= 0.9067

Step 3: Find antilogarithm

   antilog(0.9067) ≈ 8.1

**Therefore: ∛524.8 ≈ 8.1**

**Teacher's Role:** Monitor students, emphasizing the correct sequence of steps and verifying answers.

## **Phase 4: Assessment / Evaluate (Exit Ticket)**

**Learning Outcome:** To formatively assess individual student understanding.

**Exit Ticket Questions:**

1\. Use logarithm tables to evaluate the following:

   a) 345.6 × 78.9

   b) 6284 × 92.5

   c) 0.0482 × 53.7

2\. Use logarithms to calculate:

   a) 652.3 ÷ 12.7

   b) 0.0854 ÷ 3.42

   c) 4312 ÷ 58.3

3\. Solve the following using logarithms:

   a) (78.5)³

   b) (254.6)⁴

   c) (12.75)^2.5

4\. Compute the following roots using logarithms:

   a) ∛658.4

   b) √82.6

   c) ⁴√3126

5\. A square field has an area of 18,432 square metres. Use logarithms to determine the length of one side.

6\. Use logarithm tables to evaluate:

   a) √(4.56 × 12.3) ÷ 24.7

   b) √(2.718 × 9.81) ÷ (5.432 × 3.14)

7\. The volume of a cube is 79,507 cubic centimetres. Use logarithms to find the length of one side.

**Answer Key:**

1\. Multiplication:

   a) 345.6 × 78.9 ≈ 27,268

   b) 6284 × 92.5 ≈ 581,270

   c) 0.0482 × 53.7 ≈ 2.588

2\. Division:

   a) 652.3 ÷ 12.7 ≈ 51.4

   b) 0.0854 ÷ 3.42 ≈ 0.0250

   c) 4312 ÷ 58.3 ≈ 74.0

3\. Powers:

   a) (78.5)³ ≈ 484,000

   b) (254.6)⁴ ≈ 4.20 × 10⁹

   c) (12.75)^2.5 ≈ 580

4\. Roots:

   a) ∛658.4 ≈ 8.70

   b) √82.6 ≈ 9.09

   c) ⁴√3126 ≈ 7.48

5\. Square field:

   Area \= 18,432 m², Side \= √18,432

   log(18432) \= 4.2656, ÷ 2 \= 2.1328

   antilog(2.1328) ≈ 135.8 metres

7\. Cube volume:

   Volume \= 79,507 cm³, Side \= ∛79,507

   log(79507) \= 4.9004, ÷ 3 \= 1.6335

   antilog(1.6335) ≈ 43.0 cm
