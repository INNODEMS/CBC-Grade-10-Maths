    Key Takeaway 2.10.53.

    A velocity-time graph is a graph that shows how an object’s velocity (speed with direction) changes over time.
    🔗
    A velocity-time graph displays velocity on the y-axis and time on the x-axis, where the slope indicates acceleration and the area under the graph represents displacement.
    🔗
    A horizontal line signifies constant velocity, an upward slope indicates acceleration, a downward slope indicates deceleration, and a line at zero velocity means the object is stationary.
    🔗
    Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity of an object over time. It occurs when an object speeds up, slows down, or changes direction.
    🔗
    \begin{align*} \text{Acceleration} = \amp \frac{\text{Change in velocity}}{\text{Corresponding change in time}}\\ \text{a} = \amp \frac{\text{Δv}}{\text{Δt}}\\ \text{a} = \amp \frac{\text{v - u}}{\text{t}} \end{align*}
    🔗
    Where;
    🔗
    • \(\text{a}\) = Acceleration which is measured in \(\text{m/s²}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • \(\text{Δv}\) = \(\text{v - u}\) = change in velocity which is measured in metres per second \(\text{m/s}\)
      🔗
      \(\text{v}\) is the final velocity and \(\text{u}\) is the initial velocity.
      🔗
      🔗
    • \(Δt\) = \(\text{t}\) = time taken which is measured in seconds(s)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Deceleration is negative acceleration, meaning the object is slowing down. It happens when the velocity decreases over time.
    🔗
    • In a velocity-time graph, deceleration appears as a downward-sloping line.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 2.10.56.

    A car starts from rest and accelerates to a velocity of \(40 \, \text{ m/s}\) in \(10 \,\text{ seconds}\) . It then maintains this velocity for \(15 \,\text{ seconds}\) before decelerating to rest, with the total time of motion being \(45 \,\text{ seconds}\)
    🔗
    1. Draw the velocity-time graph to represent its motion.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Find the total distance covered.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Determine the average velocity.
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Calculate the acceleration and deceleration.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    1. The velocity-time graph;
      🔗
      🔗
    2. The total distance is the area under the velocity-time graph, which consists of a trapezium.
      🔗
      • Distance during acceleration (triangle).
        🔗
        \begin{align*} \text{Area} = \amp \frac{1}{2}\times \text{ base}\times \text{ height}\\ \amp \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 40\\ = \amp 200 \,\text{ m} \end{align*}
        🔗
        🔗
      • Distance during constant velocity (rectangle)
        🔗
        \begin{align*} \text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{ height}\amp \\ \amp 15 \times 40\\ = \amp 600 \,\text{ m} \end{align*}
        🔗
        🔗
      • Distance during deceleration (triangle)
        🔗
        \begin{align*} \text{Area} = \amp \frac{1}{2}\times \text{ base}\times \text{ height}\\ \amp 20 \times 40\\ = \amp 400\,\text{ m} \end{align*}
        🔗
        🔗
      • Total distance:
        🔗
        \(200+600+400\) = \(1200\,\text{ m}\)
        🔗
        Therefore, the total distance is \(1200\,\text{ m}\)
        🔗
        🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Average Velocity
      🔗
      \begin{align*} \text{Average velocity} = \amp \frac{\text{Total distance}}{\text{ Total time}}\\ = \amp \frac{1200}{45}\\ \amp = 26.67\,\text{ m/s} \end{align*}
      🔗
      Therefore, the average velocity is \(26.67\,\text{ m/s}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Acceleration and Deceleration
      🔗
      Using the equation:
      🔗
      \begin{align*} \text{a} = \amp \frac{\text{v - u}}{\text{t}} \end{align*}
      🔗
      Where;
      🔗
      \(\text{v}\) = is the final velocity (\(40 \, \text{ m/s}\text{,}\) from the graph)
      🔗
      \(\text{u}\) = is the initial velocity (\(0 \, \text{ m/s}\))
      🔗
      \(\text{t}\) = is the time taken during acceleration (\(10 \, \text{ seconds}\)).
      🔗
      \begin{align*} \amp \frac{40 - 0}{10}\\ = \amp 4 \,\text{ m/s²} \end{align*}
      🔗
      Therefore, acceleration is \(4 \,\text{ m/s²}\)
      🔗
      Under deceleration;
      🔗
      \(\text{v}\) is the final velocity (\(0 \, \text{ m/s}\text{,}\) since the car comes to rest),
      🔗
      \(\text{u}\) is the initial velocity (\(40 \, \text{ m/s}\text{,}\) from the graph)
      🔗
      \(\text{t}\) is the time taken during deceleration (\(10 \, \text{ seconds}\)).
      🔗
      \begin{align*} \amp \frac{0 - 40}{10}\\ = \amp -4 \,\text{ m/s²} \end{align*}
      🔗
      Therefore, the object is decelerating at \(4 \,\text{ m/s²}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗
